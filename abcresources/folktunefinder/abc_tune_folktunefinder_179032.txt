H: Nonesuch was built in 1538 by Henry VIII over the demolished property of the village of Cuddington,
H: near Epsom Wells in Sussex, to be the most ostentatious hunting lodge ever made.  At the very end
H: of her life, Elizabeth I visited Nonesuch, as a guest of Lord Lumley, son-in-law of the Earl of
H: Arundel, and it was reported that "there is much dancing of country dances in the privy chamber
H: at Nonesuch, before the Queen's majesty, who is exceedingly pleased therewith".
